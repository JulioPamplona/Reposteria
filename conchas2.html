INGREDIENTS:
Shell coverage:

* 200 g of wheat flour
* 200 g of sugar glass
* 200 g of shortening cut into small cubes
* 30 g of cocoa
* 15 ml of vanilla extract

Dough:

* 1 kg of wheat flour
* 15 g of salt
* 25 g of baking powder
* 550 ml of milk
* 200 g of eggs
* 15 ml of vanilla extract
* 175 g of sugar
* 150 g of butter cut into small cubes, at room temperature
* 60 g of lard



preparation:

Shell coverage:

* Sift the wheat flour with the icing sugar in a bowl. Add the vegetable shortening and mix with your hands until you obtain a smooth and homogeneous mass.
* Divide the dough into 2 portions and place them in separate containers; add to one the cocoa and to the other the vanilla extract. Knead both masses independently until you get a uniform color in each one. Cover them with adhesive plastic and reserve them.

Dough:

* Make the dough following the procedure of the biscuit dough; Add the vanilla extract after adding the eggs and replace the margarine with the butter and the lard.
* Lightly grease a bowl, place the dough in it and cover it with sticky plastic. Let the dough rest in refrigeration for at least 4 hours.

Finished:

* Remove the dough from the refrigerator, flour a work table, place the dough on top and put it on. Divide it in 70 grams portions and let them rest for 10 minutes. Bolélas and place them in the trays, separated by a few centimeters.
* Lightly squeeze each dough bun with the palms of your hands, add a little vegetable fat and spread it over each one.
* Sprinkle a little flour on the table, place the cover for cochas and knead it. Form spheres 3 centimeters in diameter and flatten them with your hands, lightly floured, to obtain thin disks.
* Cover the dough buns with the cover disks and press each with the shell marker to create a design similar to that of a sea shell. Let them rest until they double their volume. Preheat the oven to 170 ° C.
* Bake the shells for 18 minutes or until the base is golden brown. Remove them from the oven and let them cool on the rack.
